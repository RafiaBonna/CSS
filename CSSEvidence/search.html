Fiction: "The Catcher in the Rye" by J.D. Salinger – A classic coming-of-age novel about a disillusioned teenager named Holden Caulfield. "To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee – A powerful novel about racial injustice and the loss of innocence in the Deep South. "1984" by George Orwell – A dystopian tale of totalitarian control and surveillance that’s eerily relevant today. "Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen – A timeless romance with sharp social commentary. "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald – A look at the American Dream, excess, and disillusionment during the Jazz Age. Fantasy/Sci-Fi: "The Hobbit" by J.R.R. Tolkien – An adventurous prequel to "The Lord of the Rings" full of rich world-building and timeless themes. "Dune" by Frank Herbert – A science fiction epic about politics, religion, and ecology on the desert planet of Arrakis. "The Night Circus" by Erin Morgenstern – A beautifully written, magical realism novel about a mysterious circus and a competition between two magicians. "Ender's Game" by Orson Scott Card – A young genius is trained for war in space in a story about leadership, strategy, and morality.
